Gold prices dropped below $5,050 per ounce on Friday as a strengthening US dollar and fading expectations for interest rate cuts outweighed the traditional safe-haven status of gold. The yellow metal is now down for a second straight week, but still sitting more than $2,100 above where it was a year ago. The current record high of gold was achieved on January 28, 2026, at $5,602.22 per troy ounce.
